Construction of GOLD-R BioAge: Methodology

The GOLD-R BioAge was constructed using a multi-step, residual-informed framework grounded in the Gompertz law of mortality. The process involved the following key steps:

1 Modeling Underlying Mortality Risk

First, the underlying mortality risk (h) was modeled according to the Gompertz law as a function of chronological age (CA):

$$ h = \text{Rate} \cdot \exp(\text{Shape} \cdot \text{CA}) $$

2 Predicting Mortality Risk from Omics Data

A LASSO regression model with 5-fold cross-validation was trained to predict the log-transformed mortality risk (log h) using omics data (e.g., proteomics, DNA methylation levels):

$$ \log h = \text{Coef} \cdot \text{Biomarkers} + \text{Intercept} $$
(1)

This predicted mortality risk was then used to define a primary BioAge by inverting the Gompertz equation:

$$ \log h = \log(\text{Rate}) + \text{Shape} \cdot \text{BioAge} $$
(2)

3 Capturing the Residual Discrepancy

The discrepancy between the methylome-predicted and demographically expected mortality risk was captured as a residual term:

$$ \text{Residual} = \log h - \text{Shape} \cdot \text{CA} = \text{Coef}' \cdot \text{Biomarkers} + \text{Intercept}' $$
(3)

A second LASSO model was developed to predict this residual directly from omics data.

4 Deriving GOLD-R BioAge with Predicted Residual

The GOLD-R BioAge was then derived by incorporating the predicted residual (PR) back into the Gompertz framework:

$$ \text{GOLD-R BioAge} = \text{CA} + \frac{1}{\text{Shape}} (\text{PR} - \log(\text{Rate})) $$
(4)

5 Scaling Transformation for Residual Underestimation

To correct for the underestimation of biological age residuals resulting from the reduced variance of the predicted residuals compared to the original residual risks (RR), a scaling transformation was applied. The predicted residuals were standardized as follows:

$$ \text{PR}' = \text{PR} \times \left[ \frac{\text{SD}(\text{RR})}{\text{SD}(\text{PR})} \right] $$

This scaled value was then centered to the mean of the original residuals:

$$ \text{PR}' = \text{PR}' - \text{Mean}(\text{PR}') + \text{Mean}(\text{RR}) $$

The final GOLD-R BioAge was subsequently calculated using the transformed residual (PR′):

$$ \text{GOLD-R BioAge} = \text{CA} + \frac{1}{\text{Shape}} (\text{PR}' - \log(\text{Rate})) $$
(5)

Special Handling for NHANES and HRS Cohorts

For the NHANES and HRS cohorts, where raw DNA methylation data was unavailable, the residual was derived indirectly from established epigenetic clocks:

  • A composite DNAmAge was calculated from the first five principal components of seven epigenetic clocks regressed on chronological age.
  • The residual was computed using formula (3), where log h = log(Rate) + Shape ∙ DNAmAge.
  • This residual was then predicted using a panel of clinical biomarkers via a LASSO model.
  • The final biological age, termed GOLD-R DNAmCli Age, was derived using the above formulas (4) and (5).
